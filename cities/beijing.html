City Overview

Imperial Grandeur & Modern Marvel

Beijing, the capital of the People's Republic of China, stands as one of humanity's greatest urban achievements—a city where over 3,000 years of history coexists with cutting-edge modernity. As the political, cultural, and educational center of the world's most populous nation, Beijing has served as the capital for the Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties, as well as the current republic, giving it a depth of historical significance matched by few cities on Earth. With a population exceeding 21 million, this megacity sprawls across the North China Plain, anchored by the monumental Forbidden City at its heart and ringed by the Great Wall, which snakes across the mountains to the north.

The city's urban fabric tells the story of China's journey through time. The historic core, with its north-south axis aligned according to ancient cosmological principles, contains the world's largest palace complex in the Forbidden City, along with the Temple of Heaven where emperors prayed for bountiful harvests. The hutongs—narrow alleyways lined with traditional courtyard houses—offer intimate glimpses into old Beijing life, where residents still live much as they have for centuries. Yet just beyond these ancient neighborhoods rise architectural marvels of the 21st century: the CCTV Headquarters, nicknamed "Big Pants" for its distinctive shape; the National Stadium, known as the "Bird's Nest"; and the vast network of modern infrastructure that supports one of the world's most dynamic economies.

Beijing's cultural offerings are equally impressive, with over 150 museums, numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and a thriving contemporary arts scene centered in the 798 Art District. The city is home to China's most prestigious universities, including Peking University and Tsinghua University, which have produced generations of China's leaders and intellectuals. The culinary landscape spans from imperial court cuisine to street food delicacies like Beijing duck, jianbing, and zhajiangmian. Whether you're watching the sunrise over Tiananmen Square, hiking an unrestored section of the Great Wall, or exploring the hip cafes of Sanlitun, Beijing offers an endless array of experiences that capture the essence of China's past, present, and future.

A Taste of Beijing

Peking Duck (北京烤鸭)

The quintessential Beijing dish, famous for its crispy skin and tender meat, often served with pancakes and sweet bean sauce.

Iconic Poultry Must-Try

Zhajiangmian (炸酱面)

"Fried sauce noodles" - a hearty dish of thick wheat noodles topped with a rich, savory soybean paste and fresh vegetables.

Local Favorite Noodles Savory

Jiaozi (饺子)

Chinese dumplings, a staple in Northern China. Enjoy them boiled, steamed, or pan-fried with various fillings.

Getting Around

Subway

The Beijing Subway is extensive, efficient, and the best way to navigate the city and avoid traffic.

Taxis & Ride-Hailing

Taxis are plentiful, and ride-hailing apps like Didi Chuxing are very convenient for direct routes.

Bikes

Bike-sharing is ubiquitous and a great way to explore smaller streets and hutongs at your own pace.

3-Day Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Imperial Heart

  • AM

    Tiananmen Square & Forbidden City

    Start at the world's largest public square, then enter the magnificent Forbidden City.

  • PM

    Jingshan Park & Hutong Tour

    Climb Jingshan Hill for a panoramic view of the Forbidden City, then explore the nearby hutongs by rickshaw.

  • Eve

    Peking Duck Dinner

    Indulge in an authentic Peking Duck feast at a renowned restaurant.

Day 2: The Great Wall & Olympic Spirit

  • All Day

    Mutianyu Great Wall

    Take a day trip to a beautifully restored section of the Great Wall. Enjoy the cable car and toboggan ride.

  • Eve

    Olympic Park

    Visit the 2008 Olympic venues, the "Bird's Nest" stadium and "Water Cube," beautifully lit up at night.

Day 3: Temples & Palaces

  • AM

    Temple of Heaven

    Visit the serene park and marvel at the architectural masterpiece where emperors prayed for good harvests.

  • PM

    Summer Palace

    Explore the vast and beautiful imperial garden, with its lakes, temples, and pavilions.

  • Eve

    Sanlitun for Dinner & Drinks

    Experience modern Beijing's vibrant nightlife in the trendy Sanlitun area.
