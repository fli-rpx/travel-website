Chongqing

Mountain City & Hot Pot Capital

Overview

The Mountain City

Chongqing defies conventional urban planning. Built on mountains at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers, this sprawling megacity of over 30 million people has earned nicknames like "Mountain City," "City of Bridges," and "Fog City." As one of China's four direct-controlled municipalities, Chongqing encompasses an area the size of Austria, making it the largest city proper in the world by population. The city's dramatic topography has created a unique urban landscape where skyscrapers perch on cliff edges, roads wind through tunnels and over bridges, and the light rail system passes directly through residential buildings â€” a testament to human ingenuity adapting to challenging terrain.

The history of Chongqing stretches back over 3,000 years, but its modern significance began during World War II when it served as China's provisional capital during the Japanese invasion. The city endured brutal bombing campaigns yet emerged as a symbol of Chinese resilience. Today, visitors can explore this history at the Three Gorges Museum and various wartime sites scattered throughout the city. Chongqing's strategic location made it the starting point for the famous Three Gorges cruise, one of the world's great river journeys, which takes travelers through spectacular scenery to the massive Three Gorges Dam.

Chongqing's culinary scene is legendary throughout China, centered on the fiery, numbing flavors of Sichuan cuisine. The city claims to be the birthplace of hot pot, that communal dining experience where diners cook ingredients in bubbling, chili-laden broth at their tables. The local saying "If you haven't eaten Chongqing hot pot, you haven't been to Chongqing" speaks to the dish's cultural importance. Beyond hot pot, the city's street food culture is extraordinary, with night markets offering everything from spicy noodles to grilled skewers. The Hongya Cave, with its stilted architecture cascading down the riverside illuminated in golden light, has become an iconic symbol of Chongqing's unique blend of tradition and modernity, attracting millions of visitors who come to experience this city like no other.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Hongya Cave, Ciqikou Ancient Town, Yangtze River Cableway, Jiefangbei CBD, Three Gorges Museum

Local Cuisine

Chongqing Hot Pot, Spicy Noodles, Jiangtuan Fish, La Zi Ji (Spicy Chicken)

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) with comfortable temperatures

Local Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes

Chongqing Hot Pot: The most famous dish from Chongqing, characterized by its spicy and numbing broth with Sichuan peppercorns and dried chilies.

Spicy Noodles: Local noodles served in a spicy, flavorful broth with various toppings.

Jiangtuan Fish: A local fish specialty from the Yangtze River, typically prepared with pickled vegetables.

La Zi Ji (Spicy Chicken): Diced chicken stir-fried with dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns.

Best Food Streets

Ciqikou Ancient Town: Traditional snacks and street food in a historic setting.

Jiefangbei Food Street: Modern food court with hot pot restaurants and local specialties.

Nanbin Road: Riverside dining area with scenic views and various restaurants.

Transportation

Getting There

By Air: Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport (CKG) with domestic and international flights.

By Train: Major railway hub with high-speed rail connections to Chengdu, Beijing, Shanghai, and other cities.

By Bus: Extensive long-distance bus network connecting to nearby provinces.

By River: Yangtze River cruise ships from cities like Yichang and Wuhan.

Getting Around

Metro: Chongqing's metro system includes both subway and monorail lines, covering major areas.

Bus: Comprehensive bus network, though routes can be complex due to the mountainous terrain.

Taxi & Ride-Sharing: Taxis and services like DiDi are widely available.

Cable Car: Unique Yangtze River cableway offering scenic views of the city.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: City Center and Hot Pot

Morning: Visit Jiefangbei CBD and experience the modern city center.

Afternoon: Explore Hongya Cave, a stilted building complex with shops and restaurants.

Evening: Enjoy authentic Chongqing hot pot at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Culture and History

Morning: Visit Ciqikou Ancient Town, a preserved historic area with traditional architecture.

Afternoon: Take the Yangtze River cableway for panoramic city views.

Evening: Stroll along the riverfront and enjoy the illuminated cityscape.

Day 3: Museums and Nature

Morning: Visit the Three Gorges Museum to learn about the region's history and culture.

Afternoon: Explore Eling Park for scenic views and the famous "Mountain City" vista.

Evening: Experience local nightlife and try more spicy local dishes.
