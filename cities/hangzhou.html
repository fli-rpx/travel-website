Hangzhou

Ancient Capital & Modern Metropolis

Overview

Heaven on Earth

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, has long been celebrated as the most beautiful city in China, a place so enchanting that it inspired the famous saying, "Above there is heaven, below there are Suzhou and Hangzhou." For over a thousand years, this city on the banks of West Lake has served as a retreat for emperors, poets, and scholars seeking inspiration from its misty mountains, tranquil waters, and elegant gardens. The city's landscape, dominated by the UNESCO World Heritage Site of West Lake, represents the pinnacle of classical Chinese aesthetics, where nature and human artistry achieve perfect harmony.

The history of Hangzhou reaches back over 2,200 years, but its golden age came during the Southern Song Dynasty (1127-1279) when it served as the imperial capital. During this period, Hangzhou became the largest and most prosperous city in the world, a cosmopolitan center of culture, commerce, and innovation that attracted traders and travelers from across Asia and beyond. Marco Polo, who visited in the 13th century, described it as "the finest and most splendid city in the world," praising its grand canals, bustling markets, and refined culture. This legacy of elegance and sophistication continues to define Hangzhou's character today.

Beyond its natural beauty, Hangzhou is renowned for its contributions to Chinese culture, particularly in tea and cuisine. The hills surrounding West Lake produce Longjing (Dragon Well) tea, widely considered the finest green tea in China, with a history of cultivation stretching back over 1,200 years. The city's culinary tradition, known as Hangbang cai, emphasizes fresh, local ingredients and delicate flavors, with dishes like Dongpo Pork and West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy representing the height of Jiangnan cuisine. Today, Hangzhou has also emerged as a center of technological innovation, home to Alibaba and other major tech companies, creating a fascinating blend of ancient tradition and cutting-edge modernity that makes it one of China's most captivating destinations.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

West Lake, Lingyin Temple, Six Harmonies Pagoda, Su Causeway, Bai Causeway, Feilai Feng grottoes

Local Cuisine

Dongpo Pork, West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy, Longjing Tea, Beggar's Chicken, Hangzhou Yogurt

Best Time to Visit

Autumn (September-October) with clear skies and pleasant temperatures, or Spring (March-May) for blooming flowers

Local Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes

Dongpo Pork: Braised pork belly named after the Song Dynasty poet Su Dongpo, known for its tender texture and rich flavor.

West Lake Fish in Vinegar Gravy: Fresh fish from West Lake served with a sweet and sour vinegar sauce.

Longjing Tea Shrimp: Fresh shrimp stir-fried with Longjing tea leaves, combining seafood with the famous local tea.

Beggar's Chicken: Chicken wrapped in lotus leaves and clay, then baked until tender and aromatic.

Best Food Streets

Hefang Street: Traditional snacks and street food in a historic setting with traditional architecture.

West Lake Area: Lakeside restaurants serving fresh fish and local specialties with scenic views.

Qinghefang Historic District: Family-run establishments offering authentic Hangzhou cuisine.

Transportation

Getting There

By Air: Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport (HGH) with domestic and international flights.

By Train: Major high-speed rail hub connecting to Shanghai, Guangzhou, Xi'an, and other cities.

By Bus: Extensive long-distance bus network connecting to nearby provinces and cities.

Getting Around

Metro: Hangzhou's metro system provides efficient transportation to major attractions and districts.

Bicycle: Renting a bicycle offers the best way to explore West Lake's perimeter with dedicated bike lanes.

Bus: Comprehensive public bus network covering the urban area and tourist attractions.

Boat: Traditional wooden boats on West Lake provide scenic transportation and tours.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: West Lake and Culture

Morning: Explore West Lake by bicycle, visiting Su Causeway and Bai Causeway.

Afternoon: Visit Lingyin Temple and Feilai Feng grottoes.

Evening: Enjoy dinner at a lakeside restaurant with West Lake fish.

Day 2: History and Tea

Morning: Visit Six Harmonies Pagoda for panoramic views.

Afternoon: Explore Longjing tea plantations and participate in a tea ceremony.

Evening: Stroll through Hefang Street for shopping and street food.

Day 3: Modern Hangzhou

Morning: Visit the National Silk Museum to learn about silk production.

Afternoon: Explore Xixi National Wetland Park for natural scenery.

Evening: Experience modern Hangzhou at the Cloud Town tech district.
