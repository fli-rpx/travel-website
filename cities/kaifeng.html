Kaifeng

Ancient Capital of Seven Dynasties

Overview

Kaifeng, one of China's most historically significant cities, served as the capital of seven different dynasties and was once the largest city in the world during the Northern Song Dynasty (960-1127 AD). Located in eastern Henan province along the southern banks of the Yellow River, Kaifeng was known as Dongjing (Eastern Capital) during its golden age, when its population exceeded one million people and it became the political, economic, and cultural center of China. The city's strategic location at the intersection of major trade routes made it a thriving metropolis where merchants from across Asia converged, creating a cosmopolitan atmosphere that rivaled any city of its time.

The city's golden age during the Northern Song Dynasty represents one of the most sophisticated periods in Chinese urban development. Kaifeng was the first Chinese capital to break free from the rigid grid pattern of earlier cities, developing organically along the Yellow River with a more natural layout. The famous painting "Along the River During the Qingming Festival" by Zhang Zeduan depicts the bustling commercial activity of Kaifeng during this period, showing crowded markets, busy docks, and elaborate architecture that demonstrated the city's prosperity.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Daxiangguo Temple, Iron Pagoda, Dragon Pavilion, Kaifeng Museum, Jewish Quarter, Millennium City Park

Local Cuisine

Kaifeng Soup Dumplings, Sesame Cakes, Fried Dough Twists, Muslim Quarter specialties

Best Time to Visit

Autumn (September-November) for the Chrysanthemum Festival and pleasant weather

Local Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes

Kaifeng Soup Dumplings: Delicate steamed dumplings with rich broth and savory filling, different from Shanghai's version with thinner skin.

Sesame Cakes: Traditional flatbread topped with sesame seeds, crispy on the outside and soft inside.

Fried Dough Twists: Sweet and crispy twisted dough snacks, perfect for breakfast or snacks.

Muslim Quarter Specialties: Halal dishes reflecting the city's multicultural heritage including lamb skewers and noodle dishes.

Best Food Streets

Drum Tower Night Market: Historic evening market with traditional snacks and local specialties operating from dusk until late.

Muslim Quarter: Authentic halal cuisine and traditional Kaifeng snacks in a culturally rich setting.

Old Town Area: Family-run restaurants serving traditional recipes passed down through generations.

Transportation

Getting There

By Air: Zhengzhou Xinzheng International Airport (CGO) is 70km away, with high-speed rail connections to Kaifeng (30 minutes).

By Train: Kaifeng has high-speed rail connections from Zhengzhou, Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities.

By Bus: Long-distance bus services connect Kaifeng to nearby cities and provinces.

Getting Around

Walking: The historic city center is compact and ideal for walking exploration, with most attractions within walking distance.

Cycling: Bicycle rental is popular due to the city's flat terrain and bike-friendly streets.

Local Bus: Efficient bus system connects all major sites with clear signage in Chinese and English.

Taxi: Readily available and affordable for longer distances or when carrying luggage.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Imperial Heritage

Morning: Visit Daxiangguo Temple, one of China's most important Buddhist temples with stunning architecture.

Afternoon: Explore the Iron Pagoda, a historic landmark offering panoramic views of the city.

Evening: Stroll through the Drum Tower area and experience the famous night market culture.

Day 2: History and Culture

Morning: Tour the Dragon Pavilion and learn about Kaifeng's imperial past as capital of seven dynasties.

Afternoon: Visit the Kaifeng Museum to understand the city's rich history and cultural significance.

Evening: Explore the Jewish Quarter and learn about Kaifeng's unique Jewish community history.

Day 3: Gardens and Markets

Morning: Visit Millennium City Park, a recreation of Song Dynasty Kaifeng with traditional performances.

Afternoon: Shop for traditional crafts and local specialties in the old town area.

Evening: Enjoy a traditional Chinese opera performance and sample more local cuisine.
