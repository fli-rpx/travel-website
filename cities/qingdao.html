Qingdao

Where East Meets West

Overview

Qingdao is a major port city in Shandong Province, famous for its European architecture, beautiful coastline, and Tsingtao Beer. Originally a German concession, the city blends Chinese and Western influences with its red-roofed buildings, Catholic churches, and coastal scenery.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Zhanqiao Pier, Badaguan Scenic Area, Tsingtao Beer Museum, Laoshan Mountain

Local Cuisine

Seafood, Tsingtao Beer, Braised Abalone, Clams in Black Bean Sauce

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-June) and Autumn (September-October) with mild temperatures and clear skies

Travel Tips

  • Visit the Tsingtao Beer Museum to learn about the brewing process and enjoy fresh beer
  • Walk along Badaguan Scenic Area to see the unique architectural styles from different countries
  • Take a cable car up Laoshan Mountain for stunning coastal views
  • Try fresh seafood at the local markets and restaurants near the pier

Quick Facts

Basic Info

Population: 10.07 million

Province: Shandong Province

Language: Mandarin, Jiaoliao Mandarin

Timezone: China Standard Time (UTC+8)

Getting There

Airport: Qingdao Jiaodong International Airport (TAO)

Train: High-speed rail from Jinan, Yantai, Weihai (2-4 hours)

Best Transport: Metro, buses, and taxis for city travel; ferries for island visits
