Shenzhen

China's Innovation Capital & Technology Hub

Overview

Shenzhen is a major city in Guangdong Province, adjacent to Hong Kong. Once a small fishing village, it transformed into a global technology hub and special economic zone. Shenzhen is known for its modern architecture, innovation centers, and theme parks.

Today, Shenzhen stands as a testament to China's rapid economic development, featuring some of the world's most innovative tech companies and a vibrant startup ecosystem.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Window of the World, Splendid China Folk Village, Dafen Oil Painting Village, OCT-LOFT Creative Culture Park, Shenzhen Museum, Lianhuashan Park

Local Cuisine

Fresh seafood, authentic Cantonese dim sum, international fusion cuisine, street food markets, and innovative dining experiences

Best Time to Visit

Winter (November-February) offers cool, dry weather perfect for exploring. Spring and autumn are also pleasant with moderate temperatures.

Travel Tips

  • Visit the technology markets in Huaqiangbei for electronics and gadgets at competitive prices
  • Explore creative art districts like OCT-LOFT and Dafen Oil Painting Village for local art
  • Take a day trip to nearby beaches at Dameisha and Xiaomeisha for relaxation
  • Experience the city's vibrant nightlife in Futian and Nanshan districts
  • Use the efficient metro system to navigate the city quickly and affordably
  • Consider purchasing a Shenzhen Tong card for convenient transportation payments

Quick Facts

Basic Info

Population: 17.56 million
Province: Guangdong
Language: Mandarin (official), some Cantonese
Timezone: China Standard Time (UTC+8)

Getting There

Airport: Shenzhen Bao'an International (SZX)
Train: High-speed rail from Guangzhou, Hong Kong
Best Transport: Metro, buses, taxis
Ferry: Available to Hong Kong and Macau
