Tianjin

A port city with a blend of Chinese and Western architecture.

Overview

Tianjin is a major port city in northeastern China. Once home to foreign concessions, the city has a cosmopolitan feel, with a mix of European-style buildings and traditional Chinese architecture. The Hai River flows through the city, and its banks are a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Five Great Avenues, Tianjin Eye, Ancient Culture Street

Local Cuisine

Goubuli buns, Mahua (fried dough twists), Erduoyan fried cakes

Best Time to Visit

Spring and Autumn, with mild and pleasant weather.

Local Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes

Goubuli Buns: Famous steamed buns with savory fillings, a Tianjin specialty.

Mahua: Fried dough twists, crispy and sweet, perfect for snacking.

Erduoyan Fried Cakes: Traditional fried cakes with various fillings.

Best Food Streets

Ancient Culture Street: Traditional snacks and local delicacies.

Nanshi Food Street: Wide variety of Tianjin specialties.

Hai River Banks: Riverside restaurants with beautiful views.

Transportation

Getting There

By Air: Tianjin Binhai International Airport (TSN) with domestic and international flights.

By Train: High-speed rail connections from Beijing (30 minutes), Shanghai, and other major cities.

By Car: Well-connected highway system, about 1.5 hours from Beijing.

Getting Around

Metro: Efficient metro system covering major attractions.

Bus: Extensive bus network throughout the city.

Taxi: Readily available and affordable.

Walking: Many attractions are within walking distance in the city center.

Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: Historical Tianjin

Morning: Visit the Five Great Avenues to see European-style architecture.

Afternoon: Explore Ancient Culture Street for traditional crafts and snacks.

Evening: Take a boat ride on the Hai River for beautiful night views.

Day 2: Modern Tianjin

Morning: Ride the Tianjin Eye Ferris wheel for panoramic city views.

Afternoon: Visit the Tianjin Museum to learn about the city's history.

Evening: Enjoy local cuisine at Nanshi Food Street.

Day 3: Culture and Shopping

Morning: Visit the Porcelain House, a unique architectural wonder.

Afternoon: Shop at Binjiang Road pedestrian street.

Evening: Experience Tianjin's nightlife in the Italian Style Town.
