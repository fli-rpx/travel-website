Tianjin

A port city with a blend of Chinese and Western architecture.

Overview

Tianjin is a major port city in northeastern China. Once home to foreign concessions, the city has a cosmopolitan feel, with a mix of European-style buildings and traditional Chinese architecture. The Hai River flows through the city, and its banks are a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Five Great Avenues, Tianjin Eye, Ancient Culture Street

Local Cuisine

Goubuli buns, Mahua (fried dough twists), Erduoyan fried cakes

Best Time to Visit

Spring and Autumn, with mild and pleasant weather.

Travel Tips

  • Take a boat trip on the Hai River for stunning city views.
  • Explore the different architectural styles in the Five Great Avenues.
  • Bargain for souvenirs and try local snacks on Ancient Culture Street.
  • Use the metro system for convenient travel around the city.

Quick Facts

Basic Info

Population: Over 15 million

Province: Tianjin Municipality

Language: Mandarin, Tianjin dialect

Timezone: China Standard Time (UTC+8)

Getting There

Airport: Tianjin Binhai International Airport (TSN)

Train: High-speed rail from Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities.

Best Transport: Metro, taxi
