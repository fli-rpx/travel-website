Wuxi

The Pearl of Taihu Lake

Overview

Wuxi is an industrial city in Jiangsu Province, situated on the shores of Taihu Lake. Known as the 'Pearl of Taihu Lake,' Wuxi combines natural beauty with economic prosperity, featuring beautiful lake scenery, ancient water towns, and significant Buddhist sites.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Lingshan Grand Buddha, Taihu Lake, Huishan Ancient Town, Nanchang Street

Local Cuisine

Wuxi Spareribs, Yangcheng Lake Crab, Braised Noodles

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) for pleasant weather and blooming flowers

Travel Tips

  • Visit the Lingshan Grand Buddha, one of the largest Buddha statues in China
  • Take a boat tour on Taihu Lake to see the Three Immortals Penglai Island
  • Explore Huishan Ancient Town for traditional architecture and clay figurines
  • Try Wuxi's famous sweet and savory spareribs at a local restaurant

Quick Facts

Basic Info

Population: 7.46 million

Province: Jiangsu Province

Language: Mandarin, Wu Chinese (Wuxi dialect)

Timezone: China Standard Time (UTC+8)

Getting There

Airport: Sunan Shuofang International Airport (WUX)

Train: High-speed rail from Shanghai, Suzhou, Nanjing (0.5-2 hours)

Best Transport: Metro, buses, and taxis; boats for lake tours
