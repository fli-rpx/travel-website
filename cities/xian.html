Xi'an

A city where ancient history and modern vibrancy meet.

Overview

Xi'an, the ancient capital of China, is a city steeped in history. It served as the capital for 13 dynasties and is home to the world-famous Terracotta Army. Today, it's a bustling metropolis that has beautifully preserved its rich cultural heritage.

Must-See Highlights

Top Attractions

Terracotta Army, Ancient City Wall, Big Wild Goose Pagoda

Local Cuisine

Roujiamo (meat burger), Yangrou Paomo (mutton soup with flatbread), Biangbiang Noodles

Best Time to Visit

Autumn (September to November) with mild temperatures and clear skies.

Travel Tips

  • Allow at least 2-3 days to explore the main attractions.
  • The Terracotta Army is located outside the city, so plan for a half-day trip.
  • Rent a bike to cycle on the ancient city wall for a unique experience.
  • Explore the Muslim Quarter for delicious street food and souvenirs.

Quick Facts

Basic Info

Population: Approximately 12.9 million

Province: Shaanxi

Language: Mandarin, Shaanxi dialect

Timezone: China Standard Time (UTC+8)

Getting There

Airport: Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY)

Train: High-speed rail from major cities like Beijing and Shanghai.

Best Transport: Metro, buses, and taxis are convenient for getting around the city.
